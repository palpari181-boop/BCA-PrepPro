🗣️ Communication Skills (BCA Semester 1)

Chapter 1: Introduction to Communication

Communication is the process of exchanging ideas, thoughts, feelings and information between two or more people. It is a two-way process that involves a sender and a receiver.

Definition:
Communication is the process of transferring information from one person to another through a medium.

Importance of Communication:
✔ Helps in expressing ideas clearly
✔ Builds relationships
✔ Improves teamwork
✔ Essential in business and education

Elements of Communication:
• Sender
• Message
• Medium
• Receiver
• Feedback

👉 Communication is incomplete without feedback.

Chapter 2: Types of Communication

Communication can be classified into different types based on method and structure.

1. Verbal Communication:
Uses words to communicate.
Example: Speech, conversation, meetings

2. Non-Verbal Communication:
Without words, using body language.
Example: Gestures, facial expressions, eye contact

3. Written Communication:
Information is shared in written form.
Example: Emails, letters, reports

4. Visual Communication:
Uses images, charts, graphs

Chapter 3: Barriers to Communication

Barriers are obstacles that reduce the effectiveness of communication.

Types of Barriers:
✔ Physical Barriers (noise, distance)
✔ Language Barriers (different languages)
✔ Psychological Barriers (fear, emotions)
✔ Cultural Barriers (different traditions)
✔ Technological Barriers (network issues)

👉 Effective communication means removing barriers.

Chapter 4: Communication Process (Detailed)

Communication is a cyclic process involving sender and receiver.

Steps in Communication Process:
1. Sender (creates message)
2. Encoding (converting idea into message)
3. Message (information)
4. Channel (medium)
5. Receiver (gets message)
6. Decoding (understanding)
7. Feedback (response)

Chapter 5: Verbal Communication Skills

Verbal communication means communication using spoken or written words.

Types:
✔ Oral Communication (speaking)
✔ Written Communication (writing)

Advantages:
• Fast communication
• Clear expression
• Immediate feedback

Disadvantages:
• Misunderstanding possible
• No permanent record (oral)

Chapter 6: Non-Verbal Communication

Non-verbal communication is the process of communicating without words.

Types:
✔ Body Language
✔ Facial Expressions
✔ Gestures
✔ Posture
✔ Eye Contact

Importance:
• Shows emotions
• Supports verbal communication
• Builds trust

Chapter 7: Business Communication

Business communication is the exchange of information in business organizations.

Types:
✔ Internal Communication (within company)
✔ External Communication (outside company)

Examples:
• Emails
• Reports
• Meetings
• Presentations

Importance:
✔ Improves productivity
✔ Helps decision making
✔ Builds professional relationships
