📜 Chapter 2 - History of Computer

The history of computers explains how simple calculating devices gradually evolved into powerful modern computers. The journey of computers started with manual calculation tools and has reached today's Artificial Intelligence (AI) based systems.

1️⃣ Introduction

A computer is the result of continuous technological development over many centuries. Early humans used simple devices for counting and calculations. As technology improved, mechanical machines, electronic computers, and finally intelligent computers were developed.

Today, computers are used in almost every field such as education, banking, healthcare, communication, entertainment, business, engineering, and scientific research.

2️⃣ Evolution of Computers

The development of computers did not happen in a single day. It took many years of inventions and discoveries. Different scientists developed new calculating devices that became the foundation of modern computers.

The evolution of computers can be divided into different stages starting from manual calculating devices to advanced electronic computers.

3️⃣ Early Calculating Devices

📌 Abacus

The Abacus is considered the first calculating device in history. It was invented around 3000 BC in China. It consists of beads fixed on rods that are moved manually to perform arithmetic calculations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

📌 Napier's Bones

Napier's Bones was invented by John Napier in 1617. It was a calculating device made of rods marked with numbers. It was mainly used for multiplication and division.

📌 Pascaline

Pascaline was invented by Blaise Pascal in 1642. It was the first mechanical calculator capable of performing addition and subtraction using gears and wheels.

📌 Stepped Reckoner

The Stepped Reckoner was invented by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in 1673. It could perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division more efficiently than Pascaline.

4️⃣ Mechanical Computers

📌 Difference Engine

The Difference Engine was designed by Charles Babbage in 1822. It was a mechanical machine used to perform mathematical calculations automatically and accurately.

📌 Analytical Engine

The Analytical Engine was proposed by Charles Babbage in 1837. It is considered the first general-purpose computer because it had features like an Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), Memory, Input, and Output. Due to these contributions, Charles Babbage is known as the Father of Computer.

5️⃣ Electronic Computers

📌 Mark I

Mark I was developed by Howard Aiken in 1944. It was one of the first large automatic computers used for scientific calculations.

📌 ENIAC

ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) was developed in 1946. It was the first electronic digital computer and used thousands of vacuum tubes.

📌 EDVAC

EDVAC introduced the stored-program concept, allowing instructions and data to be stored in memory for faster processing.

📌 EDSAC

EDSAC was one of the first practical stored-program computers used mainly for research and education.

📌 UNIVAC I

UNIVAC I was the first commercial computer introduced in 1951. It was widely used in business and government organizations.

6️⃣ Generations of Computers

Generation Main Technology
First Generation Vacuum Tubes
Second Generation Transistors
Third Generation Integrated Circuits (IC)
Fourth Generation Microprocessors
Fifth Generation Artificial Intelligence (AI)

📚 Summary

The history of computers shows the remarkable journey from simple manual calculating devices like the Abacus to today's AI-powered computers. Every invention contributed to making computers faster, smaller, more reliable, and more powerful.
